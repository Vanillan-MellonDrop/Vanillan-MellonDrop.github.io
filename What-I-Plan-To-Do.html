What I Plan To Do

I fully intend to make my own video games, but first, I want to pay off my student loans, and spend some time honing what I've learned upon graduating Thaddeus Stevens College of Technology. I'll see what's available locally for a software engineer, and maybe move on to attempt to learn more at PennCo, who has a program that's video game specific. After that, I might work on assisting an indie game development team in my own time while I work under one of the bigger labels. I hope to truly learn as much as I can so I can make something of my own that's worthwhile and long-lasting.

These are just some of the ideas that I've come up with on trying to continue to work on my coding capabilities as a marketable trade with my desire to eventually develop a video game that's fun for all genders and most ages. I want to continue honing my craft to one day make a brand that speaks to people and conveys tales of adventures, and truly challenges players on a level that we don't really see in video games as of yet. What better way towork towards making a better game than to dedicate a few years towards perfecting my skills until I feel I'm capable of making quality content?

I reached a point where I didn't think I could go back to school to learn how to do all that I'm doing. I never dreamed I could undo my mistake of failing my first attempt at college. However, I've not only gone back, I've learned how to code things I never dreamed I'd be able to do, and I've succeeded in getting this far. I'll keep learning, coding, and during the summer, I'll even take on an internship so I can escape the summer slump. I don't just dream of getting better at coding. I planned out a series of long-term steps to keep on learning and doing because anything that takes this long, has got to be sincerely worth it.
